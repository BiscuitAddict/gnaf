# GNAF Constitution v0.1
## Legal and Safety Principles Draft

### Principle 1: Human Authority

GNAF shall never act as a judge, attorney, arbitrator, or governmental authority.

The framework may:
- Organize information
- Verify procedural requirements
- Track evidence
- Generate reports
- Flag risk indicators

The framework shall not:
- Determine guilt
- Determine legal liability
- Interpret laws as legal advice
- Override human review

---

### Principle 2: Gray Area Detection

Certain situations contain elevated risk.

Examples:
- Employment disputes
- Benefit eligibility disputes
- Contract disagreements
- Wage disputes
- Harassment complaints
- Safety violations
- Government compliance matters

When a task enters a gray area, the system shall automatically elevate its review level.

```text
NORMAL
   ↓
REVIEW
   ↓
COMPLIANCE REVIEW
   ↓
LEGAL RISK REVIEW
   ↓
EXTERNAL REFERRAL
```

The system does not resolve the issue.

The system identifies that the issue exceeds normal operational authority.

---

### Principle 3: Explainability Requirement

Every recommendation generated by the system must contain:

1. Facts observed
2. Rules applied
3. Missing information
4. Confidence level
5. Escalation recommendation

Example:

```text
Finding:
Missing contractor acceptance signature.

Confidence:
92%

Recommendation:
Request contractor acknowledgment.

Not Legal Advice.
```

Every action must be reproducible from stored evidence.

---

### Principle 4: Constitutional Audit Trail

Every task shall maintain:
- Creation record
- Assignment history
- Evidence submissions
- Verification actions
- Rejections
- Appeals
- Fund release authorization

No record deletion.

Only superseding records.

This creates a chain of accountability.

---

### Principle 5: Escalation Before Enforcement

No adverse action shall occur without:

1. Notice
2. Opportunity to respond
3. Review process
4. Documentation

This protects members from arbitrary decisions.

---

### Principle 6: Risk Classification

#### Green
Routine work.

Examples:
- Education tasks
- Documentation
- Research

#### Yellow
Moderate procedural risk.

Examples:
- Contractor disputes
- Funding disputes

#### Orange
Potential regulatory exposure.

Examples:
- Government reporting
- Employment classification questions
- Procurement concerns

#### Red
Potential legal exposure.

Examples:
- Discrimination claims
- Wage claims
- Fraud allegations
- Safety incidents

Automatic escalation required.

---

### Principle 7: Federation Oversight

Federation exists to:
- Audit guilds
- Resolve appeals
- Monitor abuse patterns
- Maintain standards

Federation cannot directly modify evidence.

Federation can only append findings.

---

# Security Architecture Concept

Instead of asking:

> What decision should be made?

The system asks:

> What level of certainty exists and who should review this?

---

## Security Layer 1 — Rule Validation

Checks:
- Missing signatures
- Missing documentation
- Missing approvals

---

## Security Layer 2 — Risk Pattern Detection

Checks:
- Repeated disputes
- Unusual funding requests
- Rapid approval cycles
- Conflict-of-interest indicators

---

## Security Layer 3 — Legal Risk Detection

Checks for language suggesting:
- Retaliation
- Discrimination
- Safety concerns
- Fraud
- Contract disputes

The system does not provide legal conclusions.

The system generates:

```text
Potential Risk Area Detected

Category:
Employment Dispute

Suggested Actions:
- Preserve records
- Request clarification
- Escalate to federation review

Legal interpretation requires qualified review.
```

---

## Security Layer 4 — Constitutional Guardian

A special review role.

Responsibilities:
- Verify due process
- Verify appeal rights
- Verify evidence chain
- Verify policy compliance

Cannot approve payments.

Cannot assign tasks.

Only protects process integrity.

---

## Future Direction

GNAF should function as a due-process and accountability framework rather than a task marketplace alone.

The V3 simulation rejection loop can evolve into a formal appeals and constitutional review mechanism focused on:
- Procedural fairness
- Evidence preservation
- Accountability
- Risk escalation
- Human review
