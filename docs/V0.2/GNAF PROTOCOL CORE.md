GNAF PROTOCOL FOUNDATION — CORE RULE SET (v0.1)



1\. IDENTITY SYSTEM

\- Guild IDs follow format:

&#x20; COUNTRY:LOCAL:BYTECODE:IDKEY

\- IDKEY is sequential, permanent, and never reused.

\- Guild names and metadata are mutable; IDKEY is immutable.

\- IDs are issued ONLY by the Federation Registry.

\- Reserved root namespace:

&#x20; COUNTRY:LOCAL:00000000:0 (GNA proprietary system).



2\. GUILD LIFECYCLE

\- Guilds are persistent networks.

\- Guilds are never deleted, only marked Dormant if inactive.

\- Guild identity remains valid even if renamed or restructured.



3\. MEMBER GOVERNANCE

\- Members may be placed on probation if owners violate federation rules.

\- Members who violate regional laws are expelled from the current guild only.

\- Expelled members cannot be forced to work with victims in the same guild.

\- Members may reapply to the same guild every 90 days (quarterly cycle).

\- Reapplications are tracked as a continuous growth history per applicant-guild pair.

\- Members retain full reputation history across all guilds.



4\. REPUTATION SYSTEM

\- Reputation is multi-dimensional (reliability, quality, safety, compliance, etc.).

\- Full reputation history is permanent and never deleted.

\- Full history is visible only to Guild Owners and Federation Auditors.

\- Public view shows only abstracted reputation indicators.

\- Expulsion and violation records are hidden from public applicants.



5\. GUILD ADMISSION RULES

\- All membership decisions are made by human Guild Owners or delegates.

\- No automated or bot-based acceptance/rejection thresholds are allowed.

\- Guild Owners may silently reject applicants using private history.

\- Applicants receive no explanation for rejection.

\- Guild decisions are based on moral and trust judgment of the owner.



6\. ECONOMIC SYSTEM

\- Guilds cannot hire non-members directly.

\- All labor occurs through guild-to-guild contracts only.

\- Guilds may contract other guilds; internal guild members execute work.

\- All contracts settle in a single federation currency: GNA-C.

\- GNA-C is issued and controlled by the Federation.



7\. MONETARY POLICY

\- GNA-C uses managed supply (inflation-controlled).

\- Currency is issued based on verified labor, infrastructure funding, and stabilization needs.

\- Currency supply may expand or contract under Federation policy.



8\. CONTRACT SYSTEM

\- Contracts begin as fixed agreements with dynamic governance layers.

\- Guilds may mutually modify contracts (scope, time, payout adjustments).

\- No unilateral changes allowed without consent from both guilds.

\- Final payout = base + adjustments + performance modifiers.



9\. DISPUTE RESOLUTION

\- All disputes are handled by a central Federation Arbitration Panel.

\- Arbitration decisions are final and non-appealable.

\- Panel structure may vary by region (permanent, rotating, or hybrid).

\- All decisions must prioritize fairness, trust stability, and network integrity.



10\. SYSTEM PHILOSOPHY

\- Guilds are autonomous execution networks.

\- Members are mobile agents within those networks.

\- Trust, safety, and fair treatment are core system priorities.

\- Economic activity reflects real labor contribution across the federation.

\- Central governance ensures stability; guild autonomy ensures scalability.
