GNAF — GUILD STRUCTURE \& ROLES (FLAT MODEL)



1\. STRUCTURAL MODEL



\- Guilds operate as flat organizations

\- No rigid hierarchy or permanent ranks

\- Leadership is functional, not positional





2\. ROLE PRINCIPLES



Roles are temporary and task-based:



\- Owner (Registry Anchor Only)

&#x20; • Handles external federation interface

&#x20; • Does NOT control daily execution



\- Members

&#x20; • Equal access to guild task board

&#x20; • Self-select tasks based on capability



\- Temporary Leads

&#x20; • Assigned per project

&#x20; • Exists only for contract duration

&#x20; • Automatically dissolves after completion





3\. TASK BOARD SYSTEM



\- Central Guild Task Board is the coordination layer

\- Members:

&#x20; • Pick tasks voluntarily

&#x20; • Propose assignments

&#x20; • Collaborate peer-to-peer



\- No forced internal hierarchy enforcement





4\. AUTHORITY MODEL



\- Authority is distributed by context:

&#x20; • Contract execution → contractor defines requirements

&#x20; • Guild execution → members self-organize

&#x20; • Verification → contractor approval + guild review optional



\- No permanent command structure inside guilds





5\. GOVERNANCE PRINCIPLE



\- Guilds are coordination networks, not corporations

\- Leadership is emergent, not assigned

\- Influence is earned through repeated contribution, not title





6\. MOBILITY RULE



\- Members can participate in multiple guilds (if allowed by guild policy)

\- Reputation follows the member across all guilds

\- Contribution history determines trust, not position





7\. SYSTEM PHILOSOPHY



\- Minimize internal bureaucracy

\- Maximize execution efficiency

\- Prevent power concentration inside guilds

\- Keep governance external (Federation layer)
